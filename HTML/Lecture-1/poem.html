At last I can be with you! The grinding hours since I left your side! The labor of being fully human, working my opposable thumb, talking, and walking upright. Now I have unclasped unzipped, stepped out of my public habit and stood in my bare feet, blessed as a tree in the night air, ready to lie down to you. Do you know when I’m alone with you I am more alone with myself than ever? I tell you, the world is a knot in my shoelace that won’t slip free, and I must sit down, kneel down, take my fingers to it and untie the knot.
